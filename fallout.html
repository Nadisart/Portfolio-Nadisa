"Fallout" Project information

  • Category: Posters; Packaging; Editorial; Graphic Motion
  • Type of project: Personal
  • Project date: February 2024 - March 2024
  • Software: Adobe Illustrator; Adobe Indesign; Adobe Photoshop; Adobe After Effects


  • Recommended Music

    ""I don't want to set the world on fire" by The Ink Spots

As it is one of my favorite video game franchises, I took inspiration and decided to create this collection. Welcome to the world of Vault-Tec! In this graphic design project, immerse yourself in the retro-futuristic aesthetic and unique atmosphere of the Fallout universe. Combining iconic elements of 1950s culture with a dystopian vision of the future, each piece in this project transports you to a world of underground shelters, advanced technology, and dark secrets. Vault-Tec, known for its safe shelters and social experiments, takes center stage, inspiring a variety of graphic creations ranging from institutional propaganda to fictional product packaging.

It's important to note that the visual identity used in this project is a tribute to the Fallout game universe and the aesthetic created by the developers at Bethesda Games Studios. I make use of the brand's logo, mascot (Vault-Boy), color palette (blue and yellow), illustrations and typography consisting of Futura, Monofonto, and Brush Script.

The recruitment posters feature captivating imagery and persuasive messaging designed to entice potential vault dwellers to secure a spot in the safety of a Vault-Tec facility. Each poster combines striking visuals with compelling text to convey the promise of safety and a brighter future underground, urging viewers to take action amidst the threat of nuclear devastation.


I recreated the Vault-Tec Survival Guide from Fallout 4, transforming the original horizontal booklet into a vertical A5-sized book. While retaining the essential information provided in the original guide, I redesigned both the cover and layout to offer a fresh perspective. By adapting the format to a more convenient vertical orientation, the guide becomes easier to handle and read.


The Vault-Tec ID card displays the resident's name and level within the vault hierarchy, providing essential identification and access privileges within the facility. Featuring a minimalist design and the iconic Vault-Tec logo, it symbolizes membership in the vault community and underscores the importance of individual roles in ensuring survival.


Emergency Instamash (an instant mashed potato product that can be easily prepared by adding water) food ration and emergency drinking water packaging. These designs offer a practical solution for survival in post-apocalyptic scenarios. Designed with simplicity and functionality in mind, they provide essential sustenance for those braving the wasteland.


And lastly, a vintage-inspired graphic motion clip showcasing Vault Boy on "Please Stand By" screens, enhanced with vintage effects and vignettes created in After Effects.
